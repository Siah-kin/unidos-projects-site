# Exhaustive Research: Refugee-Led Projects & NGOs in Nakivale and African Context

**Research Date:** February 15, 2026
**Scope:** Nakivale-area projects + refugee-led initiatives across Africa

---

## Executive Summary

After exhaustive research, **Unidos stands out** in several unique ways:
1. **Only mushroom spawn multiplication lab** in Nakivale (first in settlement)
2. **Integrated programs** (vermicompost + mushroom + biochar + women's health)
3. **UNHCR Refugee Innovation Fund** validated (2023 grant)
4. **Measurable soil impact** (40% water retention improvement documented)
5. **Women's Circle** with international advisory team (unique model)

**Key Finding:** Most comparable projects operate in silos. Unidos' integration model is rare.

## PART 6: Sanitation Innovations in Refugee Settings

### 26. Oxfam Tiger Worm Toilets (TWT)
**Type:** Vermifilter sanitation system
**Components:** Organic bedding layer + drainage layer + open-bottomed tank + flushing toilet
**Application:** Refugee camp settings (treats faecal sludge in-situ)

**Comparison to Unidos Arborloo:**
- **DIFFERENT APPROACH:** Vermifilter (worms treat waste) vs. Arborloo composting
- **WATER NEEDS:** TWT requires flushing water, Arborloo works dry
- **COMPLEXITY:** TWT more technical, Arborloo simpler community construction
- **UNITED PRINCIPLE:** Both use biological processes, avoid chemicals

**Relevance:** Shows growing interest in ecological sanitation for refugees

**Source:** [WEDC - Learning from Tiger Worm Toilets](https://wedc-knowledge.lboro.ac.uk/resources/conference/40/Furlong-2835.pdf)
