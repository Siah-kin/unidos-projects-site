## ðŸŽ¯ Executive Summary

This document introduces a pioneering research project: **transforming Unidos Social Innovation Center, a 6-time award-winning refugee-led NGO in Nakivale, Uganda, into a decentralized autonomous organization (DAO)**.

**The Challenge**: Most refugee-led NGOs fail within 3 years due to funding gaps, lack of commercial viability, and donor dependency. Traditional NGO governance concentrates power in founders/boards, making succession and scaling difficult.

**The Vision**: By 2027, Unidos operates as a hybrid DAOâ€”refugee farmers govern via cooperation scores (not token purchases), production quality determines vote weight (not wealth), and blockchain verification replaces expensive audits. **Research Questions**:
1. Can DAO governance work in low-connectivity refugee settlements? (Telegram-first, wallet-later)

4. Can award-winning NGOs stake reputation to vet newcomers? (On-chain trust transfer)
5. Can B2B revenue + Euler pool distributions achieve self-sustainability? (Exit grants by Year 3)

**Methodology**: Iterative design (pilot â†’ refine â†’ scale), grounded in 8 years Unidos field data, informed by Bonzi SIAH Protocol, validated against Ostrom commons governance and Mondragon cooperative principles.

**Core Proposition**:

**Key Transformations**:

### Why DAO Technology Specifically?

**1. Trust Without Intermediaries**
- Traditional: Donors trust UNHCR â†’ UNHCR trusts Unidos â†’ Unidos reports impact

**2. Transparent Governance**
- Traditional: Board votes behind closed doors â†’ farmers learn about decisions weeks later

- Traditional: Wealth = power (Western donors shape agenda)

- **Benefit**: Refugees who grow food, teach others, build community govern organization (not external funders)

**4. Programmable Incentives**
- Traditional: Fixed salaries (same pay regardless of quality/effort)
- DAO: Euler pool distributions (proportional to PQI score Ã— cooperation score)
- **Benefit**: High-quality producers earn 30-50% premium â†’ race to the top (not mediocrity)

**5. Composability with Global Ecosystem**
- Traditional: Unidos isolated (no integration with DeFi, impact investing, global donor networks)

### The SIAH Protocol Foundation

**What is SIAH?**
SIAH (Semantic Identity & Actionable HEROES) is a DAO governance framework developed by Bonzi (Telegram bot, regenerative finance project). Unidos adapts SIAH for refugee NGO context.

**SIAH Core Components**:

1. **HEROES Behavioral Detection** (Cooperation Scoring)
   - 6 behavior types: Helper, Educator, Reflector, Organizer, Engager, Supporter
   - AI detects patterns (answering questions, sharing knowledge, organizing tasks)
   - Points accumulate â†’ tier progression (Lurker â†’ Participant â†’ Helper â†’ Trusted â†’ Pillar â†’ MVP)
   - Cooperation score = vote weight (meritocratic governance)

2. **Production Quality Index (PQI)** (Fair Chain Verification)
   - 100-point scale (9 dimensions: size, color, contamination, moisture, timing, yield, presentation, shelf life, traceability)
   - Batch-level scoring (mushroom harvest, vermicompost batch, biochar sack)
   - Merkle proofs (cryptographic verification, on-chain anchoring)
   - Premium pricing: 90-100 = 30-50% markup, <60 = rejected

3. **Euler Pool** (Gasless Distributions)
   - O(1) gas cost (single transaction distributes to 500+ wallets)
   - Proportional to contribution (not purchase amount)
   - Example: Farmer A (PQI 85, cooperation 150) earns 1.5x farmer B (PQI 70, cooperation 80)

4. **Trust-Weighted Voting** (Governance)
   - Vote weight = cooperation score Ã— production multiplier
   - Production multiplier: PQI â‰¥85 = 1.5x, PQI 70-84 = 1.0x, PQI <70 = 0.5x
   - Example: Farmer with 200 cooperation + 85 PQI = 200 Ã— 1.5 = 300 vote weight

5. **Wizard Automation** (Onboarding, M&E, Reporting)
   - 4 flows: Donation setup, Crowdfunding, Impact verification, Task marketplace
   - 20-minute wizard vs weeks of manual reporting
   - Auto-generates Merkle proofs, donor reports, grant applications

**Why SIAH Works for Unidos**:
- **Telegram-first**: No smartphone required (works on basic phones, low connectivity)
- **Cooperation > wealth**: Earn governance by helping, not buying tokens (refugee-friendly)
- **PQI replaces WFTO**: $0 vs $2,000/year fair trade certification
- **Euler pool scalable**: 501 farmers today, 10,000 farmers tomorrow (same gas cost)

## ðŸ”¬ Research Questions

This project explores **5 critical questions** at the intersection of DAO technology, refugee livelihoods, and regenerative agriculture:

### 1. **Can DAO Governance Work in Low-Connectivity Refugee Settlements?**

**Context**:
- Nakivale: Spotty electricity (6-8 hours/day), limited internet (3G, expensive data)
- Smartphone penetration: ~30% (most have basic feature phones)
- Digital literacy: Low (most farmers 40+ years old, limited tech experience)

**Hypothesis**:
Telegram-first onboarding (accessible on basic phones, low data usage) + delayed wallet creation (only after trust established) = higher adoption than wallet-first DAOs.

**Pilot Test** (Q2 2026):
- Onboard 50 farmers to Unidos Telegram bot
- Track: Engagement rate, HEROES detection accuracy, time to first cooperation score
- Measure: % who complete onboarding, % active weekly, % transition to wallet (if needed)

**Success Criteria**:
- 80%+ complete Telegram onboarding (vs <20% wallet-first DAOs)
- 60%+ active weekly (vs ~5% typical DAO participation)
- Cooperation scores accurately reflect contribution (manual validation vs AI detection)

---

### 2. **Can Cooperation Scores Replace Token-Weighted Voting?**

**Context**:
Traditional DAOs use token-weighted voting (1 token = 1 vote). This favors wealthy early adopters, creating plutocracy. Refugees can't afford to buy tokens, so they're excluded from governance.

**Hypothesis**:
Cooperation scoring (earn votes by helping, not buying) creates meritocracyâ€”those who contribute most govern, regardless of wealth.

**Pilot Test** (Q3 2026):
- Launch Snapshot voting (cooperation-weighted, not token-weighted)
- Test proposals: Budget allocation, partner selection, quality standards
- Compare: Voter turnout, decision quality, farmer satisfaction vs traditional board votes

**Success Criteria**:
- 60%+ voter turnout (vs <5% typical DAO, 0% beneficiary participation in traditional NGO)
- Decisions align with beneficiary needs (farmer survey: "DAO votes better reflect our priorities")
- No gaming detected (HEROES AI catches Sybil attacks, ring voting, purchased engagement)

---

### 3. **Can Cryptographic Verification Replace Expensive Certification?**

**Context**:
WFTO fair trade certification costs $2,000/year + audit fees, prohibitive for refugee NGO. Annual audits create 12-month lag (can't pivot based on real-time data).

**Hypothesis**:
PQI scoring (batch-level quality tracking) + Merkle proofs (cryptographic verification) + HEROES detection (behavioral trust) = fair chain verification at $0 cost, real-time updates.

**Pilot Test** (Q1-Q2 2026):
- Score 100 mushroom batches (10 farmers, 10 batches each)
- Generate Merkle proofs (anchor to Polygon blockchain)
- Compare: PQI scores vs manual quality inspection (accuracy check)
- Survey donors: "Do you trust PQI + Merkle proofs as much as WFTO certification?"

**Success Criteria**:
- PQI accuracy â‰¥90% vs manual inspection (AI doesn't miss contamination, quality issues)
- Donors trust PQI (80%+ say "yes, would fund based on cryptographic proof alone")
- Cost: $0 vs $2,000/year (2,000x ROI)

---

### 4. **Can Award-Winning NGOs Stake Reputation to Vet Newcomers?**

**Context**:
African NGOs face blanket distrust due to fraud (ghost projects, inflated metrics, fund diversion). Legitimate refugee-led NGOs suffer (collateral damage).

**Hypothesis**:
On-chain reputation staking (Unidos stakes 50 reputation points to vet newcomer NGO) transfers trust (jury â†’ Unidos â†’ newcomer) while ensuring accountability (slashing if fraud).

**Pilot Test** (Q4 2026):
- Mint Unidos award NFTs (6 awards = 270 reputation points)
- Vet 3 newcomer NGOs (RRF, SINA, one unknown applicant)
- Stake 30-50 reputation per NGO (skin in the game)
- Track: Batting average (% succeed), donor trust (survey), slashing incidents

**Success Criteria**:
- 80%+ batting average (2-3 out of 3 vetted NGOs reach $500/month sustainability)
- Donors trust Unidos-vetted NGOs (60%+ say "would fund based on Unidos vetting alone")
- 0-1 slashing incidents (Unidos vetting is accurate, not reckless)

---

### 5. **Can B2B Revenue + Euler Pool Achieve Self-Sustainability?**

**Context**:
Traditional NGOs rely 90%+ on grants (perpetual dependency). B2B social enterprises exist (MADE51, SINA) but don't use crypto incentives (Euler pool distributions).

**Hypothesis**:
B2B revenue (mushroom spawn, vermicompost, biochar sales) provides stable baseline + Euler pool distributions (donor tips, task marketplace commissions) create upside â†’ 70/20/10 distribution model achieves self-sustainability by Year 3.

**Pilot Test** (Q2-Q4 2026):
- Secure 1-2 B2B clients (hotel, farm, NGO partners)
- Deploy Euler pool (Polygon mainnet)
- Track revenue: B2B sales, crowdfunding, Euler pool tips
- Measure: Break-even timeline, revenue diversification ratio

**Success Criteria**:
- $10K+ B2B revenue by Q4 2026 (proves commercial viability)
- 3 Euler pool distributions (proves donor engagement)
- Revenue mix: 40%+ B2B, 40% grants, 20% Euler pool (diversified, not 95% grants)
- Path to break-even: Month 18-24 projection (self-sustaining by 2027)

### Research Philosophy

**Principle 1: "No Innovation Without Implementation"**
We don't theorizeâ€”we build, test, learn, iterate. Every claim must be validated with field data.
