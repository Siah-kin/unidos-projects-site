# Bonzi Credit Union: Microfinance Model for Refugee Projects

**Research Focus**: Where does the $500 come from to sponsor a local refugee's project?

**Date**: February 16, 2026
**Status**: ✅ PUBLIC (funding model for Year 2-3 implementation)

## Executive Summary

**Question**: Where do we find $500 to sponsor a local refugee his/her project?

**Answer**: The **Bonzi Credit Union** generates loan capital from **4 revenue streams**:

1. **Data-as-Collateral Vault** (40% of capital) — $3.50-6.50/user/month from ethical data monetization
2. **Euler Pool Surplus** (30% of capital) — 10-20% of B2B revenue (mushroom spawn, biochar, vermicompost sales)
3. **Member Deposits** (20% of capital) — Optional savings accounts (earn 2-5% interest)
4. **External Grants** (10% of capital) — Impact investors, DAOs, foundations

**Target**: $100,000 loan capital by Year 2 → 200 microloans × $500 average

**Loan Terms**: 0-5% interest (based on cooperation score), 6-12 month repayment, cooperation score ≥101 required

**Creditworthiness**: Cooperation score (earned via HEROES behavioral detection) replaces traditional credit checks. Score ≥101 = 90%+ repayment rate.

### Stream 2: Euler Pool Surplus (30% of Capital)

### Stream 3: Member Deposits (20% of Capital)

**Mechanism**: Members can deposit UNIDOS tokens or cash into savings accounts, earning 2-5% interest. Credit Union uses deposits for loans (traditional credit union model).

**Target Participation**: 20% of members deposit savings (1,000 users by Year 2)

**Average Deposit**: $50/user (conservative for refugee context)

**Total Deposits**: 1,000 users × $50 = **$50,000**

**Interest Paid to Depositors**: 2-5% APR (competitive with mobile money, higher than banks)

**Why Refugees Would Deposit**:
- Higher interest than mobile money (0-1% typical)
- Peer-to-peer trust (refugee-led Credit Union)
- Access to microloans if they need it later
- Data Vault members earn tokens automatically, can deposit surplus

## Part 3: Loan Terms & Eligibility

### Eligibility Requirements

**Minimum Cooperation Score**: 101 (Helper tier)

**How to Earn Cooperation Score**:
- Complete HEROES actions (help others, share knowledge, organize events): +1-5 points per action
- Participate in surveys: +10 points per survey
- Attend training sessions: +20 points per session
- Refer new members: +15 points per verified referral
- Repay loans on time: +50 points per successful loan

**Baseline**: New member starts at 0, can reach 101 in 2-3 months with moderate engagement (20 HEROES actions + 5 surveys + 2 trainings)

**Ineligible**: Score <101 (can build score first), or previous loan default (unless rehabilitated via graduated sanctions)

---

### Loan Sizes

**Tier 1: Starter Loan** (Score 101-150)
- Amount: $100-300
- Use Case: Small inventory (mushroom bags, seeds, tools)
- Repayment: 6 months
- Interest: 0-2%

**Tier 2: Growth Loan** (Score 151-200)
- Amount: $300-700
- Use Case: Equipment (tricycle, solar dryer, welding kit)
- Repayment: 9 months
- Interest: 2-4%

**Tier 3: Expansion Loan** (Score 201+)
- Amount: $700-1,500
- Use Case: Business expansion (hire workers, rent space, bulk inventory)
- Repayment: 12 months
- Interest: 3-5%

**Zero-Interest Option**: Members with cooperation score ≥250 (Super Helper tier) qualify for 0% interest loans (funded by external grants only)

---

### Interest Rates (Risk-Adjusted by Cooperation Score)

**Why Charge Interest?**
- Cover operational costs (loan management, default risk)
- Sustainable revolving fund (interest paid back into Credit Union → more loans)
- Lower than traditional MFIs (15-30% typical) or mobile money lenders (50-100% in informal sector)

**Interest Rate Tiers**:

| Cooperation Score | Interest Rate | Rationale |
|-------------------|--------------|-----------|
| 101-150 (Helper) | 0-2% | New borrowers, moderate risk, keep rates low to encourage access |
| 151-200 (Super Helper) | 2-4% | Proven engagement, lower default risk |
| 201-250 (Community Leader) | 3-5% | Highest trust, but larger loans = higher nominal interest |
| 250+ (Champion) | 0% | Grant-funded loans for top contributors |

**Example Loan**:
- Amount: $500
- Score: 175 (Super Helper tier)
- Interest: 3% APR
- Term: 9 months
- Monthly Payment: $56.50
- Total Repayment: $509 (only $9 in interest)

**Compare to Traditional MFI**:
- Same $500 loan at 25% APR
- Total Repayment: $575 (75$ in interest) → **8x more expensive**

---

### Repayment Structure

**Repayment Method**: UNIDOS tokens or mobile money (UGX)

**Flexibility**:
- **Option 1**: Monthly payments (e.g., $56.50/month for $500 loan)
- **Option 2**: Bi-weekly payments (smaller amounts, easier for informal income)
- **Option 3**: Harvest-based payments (pay after selling crops/products)

**Grace Period**: 1 month before first payment (allows time to use loan productively)

**Early Repayment**: No penalty (encouraged, earns +10 cooperation score bonus)

**Late Payment**:
- 1-7 days late: Warning (no penalty)
- 8-14 days late: -5 cooperation score
- 15-30 days late: -10 cooperation score, flagged for support
- 30+ days late: Loan restructuring or graduated sanctions

**Graduated Sanctions** (instead of immediate default):
1. **Support First**: Unidos coordinator meets borrower, identifies issue (health crisis, crop failure, market collapse)
2. **Restructure**: Extend term (9 months → 12 months), reduce payments
3. **Community Mediation**: Peer support from Credit Union members
4. **Last Resort**: Loan write-off, cooperation score reset to 50 (can rebuild)

## Part 5: Credit Union Governance (Who Decides Who Gets Loans?)

### Decentralized Loan Approval (DAO Governance)

**Problem**: Traditional MFIs = centralized loan officers (bias, corruption risk, slow approvals)

**Solution**: Bonzi Credit Union uses **cooperation-weighted voting** (Snapshot governance) where existing members vote on loan applications.

**Loan Application Process**:

1. **Borrower Submits Application** (via Telegram bot or web form)
   - Requested amount: $500
   - Use case: "Purchase 100 mushroom bags to expand production"
   - Repayment plan: "9 months, $56.50/month from mushroom sales"
   - Cooperation score: 160 (automatically displayed)

2. **Application Published to DAO** (Snapshot proposal)
   - All Credit Union members (cooperation score ≥101) can view application
   - Voting period: 3 days
   - Vote weight = cooperation score (score 200 = 2x vote power of score 100)

3. **Community Votes**
   - **Yes**: Approve loan (majority vote)
   - **No**: Deny loan (with feedback: "build cooperation score higher first")
   - **Request More Info**: Ask borrower to clarify use case or repayment plan

4. **Automated Approval** (if vote passes)
   - Smart contract (UnidosLocalBank.sol) mints UNIDOS tokens (loan amount)
   - Borrower receives tokens in wallet (can spend in marketplace or cash out)
   - Repayment schedule starts after 1 month grace period

5. **Repayment Tracking** (on-chain)
   - Monthly payments automatically deducted from borrower's wallet (if they opt-in to auto-pay)
   - Or borrower manually sends tokens to Credit Union address
   - On-time repayment = +5 cooperation score/month
   - Late payment = -5 cooperation score, flagged for support

---

### Anti-Gaming Protections

**Problem**: What if someone creates fake accounts, earns cooperation score artificially, takes loan, and disappears?

**Protections**:

1. **Community Vouching** (Onboarding)
   - New member must be vouched for by 3 existing members (cooperation score ≥101)
   - Vouchors stake reputation: if referred member defaults, vouchors lose -10 cooperation score

2. **EigenTrust Reputation Graph**
   - Detects isolated clusters (fake accounts vouching for each other)
   - Requires diverse vouching (3 vouchors from different social networks)

3. **Behavioral Patterns** (HEROES Detection)
   - Real helpers show diverse actions (surveys + training + helping others)
   - Bots show repetitive patterns (only surveys, no social interaction)

4. **Graduated Loan Limits**
   - First loan: Maximum $300 (Tier 1), even if score is high
   - Second loan: Can access Tier 2 ($500-700) if first loan repaid
   - Third loan: Can access Tier 3 ($1,000+) if two loans repaid

5. **Phone Number Wallet** (Celo)
   - Wallet tied to unique phone number (hard to create 100 fake accounts)
   - SMS verification required

## Part 8: Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Low Repayment Rates (Below 80%)

**Scenario**: Borrowers default en masse due to external shock (drought, conflict, market crash).

**Mitigation**:
- **Emergency Fund**: 10% of loan capital held as reserve for restructuring/write-offs
- **Income Diversification**: Encourage borrowers to have 2+ income streams (farming + transport, tailoring + teaching)
- **Group Loans**: 5-person groups co-guarantee each other (peer pressure improves repayment)
- **Insurance**: Partner with microinsurance providers (Pula, ACRE Africa) to cover crop failure, health crises

**Impact**: If repayment drops to 70%, Credit Union pauses new loans, focuses on restructuring existing loans, applies for emergency grant ($50K) to recapitalize.

---

### Risk 2: Regulatory Shutdown

**Scenario**: Uganda/Kenya government bans "unlicensed lending" (Credit Union lacks official license).

**Mitigation**:
- **Partner with Licensed MFI**: Bonzi Credit Union partners with FINCA, VisionFund, or local SACCO, using their license as umbrella
- **Community-Based Organization (CBO) Status**: Register as CBO (not financial institution) to avoid banking regulations
- **Pilot First, License Later**: Operate small-scale (100 loans) to prove model, then apply for license with track record

**Impact**: If shut down, pivot to "Internal Lending Circle" (peer-to-peer loans within Unidos community, not public-facing Credit Union).

---

### Risk 3: Sybil Attacks (Fake Loan Applications)

**Scenario**: Fraudster creates 10 fake accounts, earns cooperation score via bots, applies for 10 loans, disappears.

**Mitigation**:
- **Community Vouching**: 3 real humans must vouch for new member
- **Phone Number Verification**: Celo wallet requires unique SIM card (expensive to buy 10 SIM cards)
- **In-Person Verification**: Unidos coordinator meets borrower before loan approval (for loans >$300)
- **Graduated Loan Limits**: First loan capped at $300, reduces fraud incentive

**Impact**: If Sybil attack succeeds, maximum loss = $300 per fake account. Community vouching identifies vouchors, who lose -50 cooperation score (reputational cost discourages future fraud).
