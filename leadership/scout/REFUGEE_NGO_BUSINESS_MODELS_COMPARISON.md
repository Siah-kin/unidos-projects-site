## III. Compounding Effects — Which Model Has Greatest Self-Reinforcing Growth?

### **Village Enterprise: 933% Savings Compounding (Winner — Financial Capital)**

**Mechanism**:
1. Year 0: Household in extreme poverty, near-zero savings
2. Year 1: Business training + $150 startup cash → micro-enterprise launched
3. Year 2-5: Business profits → savings accumulate (8.33x growth per year average)
4. Result: **933% increase in savings over 5 years**

**Compounding Formula**:
- Savings → capital → investment in business growth → more profit → more savings → **loop**

**Why it compounds**:
- Savings groups (rotating credit)
- Business profits reinvested
- Network effects (3-person cooperatives support each other)
- Skills training enables ongoing problem-solving

**Limitation**: Financial capital compounds, but **ecological capital depletes** (retail/services don't regenerate soil, water, biodiversity).

---

### **African Women Rising: 80%+ Yield Doubling (Winner — Ecological Capital)**

**Mechanism**:
1. Year 0: Degraded soil, low yields
2. Year 1: Permagarden training → composting → soil improvement → yields double
3. Year 2+: Healthier soil → more organic matter → better water retention → higher yields → more compost → **loop**

**Compounding Formula**:
- Healthy soil → higher yields → more organic waste → more compost → healthier soil → **loop**

**Why it compounds**:
- Soil organic matter accumulates over time (carbon sequestration)
- Perennial plants establish (less replanting effort)
- Water infiltration improves (drought resilience)
- Biodiversity increases (pest management)

**Limitation**: Subsistence farming (household food security) ≠ market sales. **Ecological capital compounds, but financial capital does not** (no B2B revenue stream).

### **Unidos Unique Advantages: Market Access + Fair Chain Optimization**

#### **1. Vetted Grant Application Funnel (SIAH Wizard Integration)**

**Problem Other NGOs Face**:
- AWR, RRF, SINA, Village Enterprise all depend on continuous grant applications
- Manual grant writing (expensive, time-consuming)
- No standardized vetting system for proving impact
- Donors have no way to verify claims

**Unidos + SIAH Solution**:
- **Wizard flow: "Prove Impact for Grant Application"**
  - Step 1: Gather data (PQI scores, HEROES cooperation scores, production logs)
  - Step 2: Generate verification trail (Merkle proofs, on-chain attestations)
  - Step 3: Auto-generate donor report (impact dashboard, photos, beneficiary testimonials)
  - Step 4: Submit grant application with cryptographic proof
- **3-layer triage assessment**:
  - ProjectStatusLayer: Is budget on track? Timeline met? Resources allocated?
  - SentimentLayer: Are farmers happy? Any complaints? Community sentiment positive?
  - ImpactLayer: Did production actually increase? Soil health improve? Income rise?
- **Vetting score (100 points)**:
  - Financial transparency (20 pts): On-chain treasury, Euler pool distribution visible
  - Output delivered (30 pts): PQI-scored mushroom/vermicompost production
  - Beneficiary impact (25 pts): 501 farmers, 88% income increase, cooperation scores
  - Sustainability (15 pts): B2B revenue, DAO governance, equipment investment
  - Quality standards (10 pts): PQI 85/100 average, UNHCR validation (Gene Dewey Award)

**Result**: Unidos becomes **easiest NGO to fund** because impact is **cryptographically verifiable**, not just self-reported.

#### **2. Fair Chain Optimization via PQI Scoring**

**Problem Other NGOs Face**:
- MADE51 uses WFTO fair trade standards (manual audits, expensive certification)
- RRF has no formal fair chain verification (trust-based)
- AWR focuses on subsistence (no market sales, no fair chain needed)
- Village Enterprise local markets (no export, no fair chain needed)

**Unidos Solution**:
- **PQI (Production Quality Index) = Fair Chain Verification System**
  - 100-point scoring across 9 dimensions (size consistency, contamination-free, moisture content, yield efficiency, etc.)
  - Automated scoring via HEROES detection + farmer self-reporting + spot checks
  - High PQI score (85+) = premium pricing justified
  - Transparent: Buyers see PQI score before purchase

**Fair Chain Formula**:
```
High PQI Score → B2B Premium Pricing → Higher Farmer Revenue
    ↓
Higher Revenue → Reinvestment in Regenerative Practices
    ↓
Better Practices → Higher PQI Score → Loop Closes
```

**Why This Beats WFTO Certification**:
- WFTO certification costs $500-2,000/year + audit fees (prohibitive for refugee NGO)
- Manual audits (slow, annual)
- Unis PQI scoring:
  - $0 cost (built into SIAH system)
  - Real-time (updated with every production batch)
  - Cryptographically verifiable (Merkle proofs, ZK attestations planned)
  - More granular (100-point scale vs binary "certified/not certified")

### **The Compounding Multiplier Effect**

**Traditional NGO Model** (AWR, RRF, most refugee NGOs):
```
Year 1: $100K grant → Train 100 farmers → Double yields → Food security ✓
Year 2: $100K grant → Train 100 more farmers → Double yields → Food security ✓
Year 3: $100K grant → Train 100 more farmers → Double yields → Food security ✓

RESULT: Linear growth. Every dollar requires new grant. No compounding.
```

**Social Enterprise Model** (SINA, Village Enterprise):
```
Year 1: $100K grant → Train 100 entrepreneurs → Launch businesses → $50K revenue
Year 2: $50K revenue (self-sustaining) → Train 50 more entrepreneurs → $75K revenue
Year 3: $75K revenue → Train 75 more → $112K revenue
Year 4: $112K revenue → Train 112 more → $168K revenue

RESULT: Financial compounding. But limited ecological/social impact (retail businesses don't regenerate ecosystems).
```

**Market Access Model** (MADE51):
```
Year 1: Connect 100 artisans to global markets → $200K in sales → $150K to artisans (75% fair wage)
Year 2: Reputation grows → 200 artisans → $400K in sales → $300K to artisans
Year 3: Brand established → 400 artisans → $800K in sales → $600K to artisans

RESULT: Financial + social compounding (artisans earn sustainable income, preserve culture). But no ecological regeneration (handicrafts don't restore soil/water/biodiversity).
```

**Unidos Regenerative DAO Model**:
```
Year 1: $50K grant + $100K crowdfunding → Train 501 farmers → Regenerative ag → Soil improves 20%
    → Mushroom spawn B2B → $48K revenue
    → Vermicompost B2B → $29K revenue
```
