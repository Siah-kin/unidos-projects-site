üìö Research Summary

This research project documents Unidos Social Innovation Center's transformation from a traditional grant-dependent NGO into a DAO-governed, self-sustaining social enterprise. The research explores 5 critical questions:

  1. Low-connectivity DAO governance: Can Telegram-first onboarding work in refugee settlements?
  2. Cooperation-weighted voting: Can meritocracy replace plutocracy (earn votes by helping, not buying)?
  3. Cryptographic verification: Can HEROES + PQI replace expensive WFTO certification ($0 vs $2K/year)?
  4. Reputation staking: Can award-winning NGOs vet newcomers via on-chain credibility transfer?
  5. Commercial self-sustainability: Can B2B revenue + Euler pool achieve break-even by Year 3?

If successful, this model can replicate to 100+ refugee-led NGOs across Africa by 2030. The research contributes to DAO governance theory (Global South DAOs), refugee entrepreneurship literature, and blockchain for impact practice.
