Since 2018: trained 3,000+ farmers in food forest design, processed 800 kg food waste weekly into worm compost, taught 700 women to grow oyster mushrooms and make organic soap.

Who We Are

Unidos Social Innovation Center

Founded in 2018 by refugees in Nakivale after WFP food rations were cut. Started with 15 people learning to grow food using permaculture methods. Now employs 35 full- and part-time staff, all refugees.

We serve over 20,000 refugees from Congo, Burundi, Somalia, Ethiopia, Eritrea, and Sudan across multiple programs: mushroom cultivation, worm composting, biochar production, women's health education, and arborloo toilet construction.

Located in Nakivale Refugee Settlement, Isingiro District, South West Uganda.
