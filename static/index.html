Who We Are

Unidos Social Innovation Center

Founded in 2018 by refugees in Nakivale after WFP food rations were cut. Started with 15 people learning to grow food using permaculture methods. Now employs 35 full- and part-time staff, all refugees.

We serve over 20,000 refugees from Congo, Burundi, Somalia, Ethiopia, Eritrea, and Sudan across multiple programs: mushroom cultivation, worm composting, biochar production, women's health education, and arborloo toilet construction.

Located in Nakivale Refugee Settlement, Isingiro District, South West Uganda.

What We Do

Our Programs

Training programs in agriculture, health education, and income generation.

Education & Skills Training

English courses, business training, and leadership development programs that equip refugees with the skills they need to thrive.

Women's Empowerment

Organic soap making, mushroom cultivation, and tree planting programs that create sustainable income for women in the community.

Regenerative Agriculture

Natural farming training, soil regeneration through vermicomposting, food forest design, and waste recycling techniques.

Food Production

Established 6 food forests promoting biodiversity while equipping new arrivals with farming tools and knowledge.

Stories of Change

Meet Mariam

Originally from the Democratic Republic of Congo and now living in Nakivale refugee camp, Mariam is a passionate soap maker who serves as Program Manager at Unidos.

She leads women's projects, teaching other refugees how to make organic soap while providing business training and networking opportunities that generate income.

"Through Unidos, I found not just a livelihood, but a purpose. Now I help other women discover their own potential."
