Our Mission

Improving Lives Through Health & Income

Women's Circle is Unidos's complete women's development initiative that combines health education, income generation, and community support to break cycles of poverty and gender-based violence in Nakivale Refugee Settlement.

We recognize that refugee women face unique challenges: limited access to health information, economic dependency, and vulnerability to violence. Women's Circle addresses these challenges across all areas through education, skills training, and peer support networks.

When women thrive, entire communities improve. We're building a future where women lead, earn, and inspire.

Impact

Why Women's Circle Matters

Breaking Poverty Cycles

Women gain skills and income to support their families, reducing dependency on aid and creating sustainable livelihoods that last for generations.

Reducing Violence

Education about rights, health, and economic independence helps women to escape abusive situations and advocate for themselves and their daughters.

Building Community

Monthly support groups create networks of mutual aid where women share knowledge, resources, and emotional support in a safe, trusted environment.

Improving Health

Access to health education reduces maternal mortality, improves child nutrition, and prevents disease, creating healthier families and communities.

Creating Leaders

Women who complete our programs become leaders in their communities, training others and advocating for women's rights and opportunities.

Changing Futures

Daughters see their mothers as business owners and community leaders, breaking gender stereotypes and inspiring the next generation.

Leadership

Founded by Mariam Antoine

Mariam Antoine

Co-founder & Women's Circle Director, Unidos

Congolese refugee and women's health advocate who co-founded Unidos and launched Women's Circle to address the urgent health and economic needs of refugee women in Nakivale.

Having experienced firsthand the challenges refugee women face, Mariam created Women's Circle as a safe space where women can learn, earn, and support each other. Under her leadership, the program has empowered 700+ women and continues to grow.

Mariam's vision: Every woman deserves access to health education, economic opportunity, and a community that supports her dreams.
