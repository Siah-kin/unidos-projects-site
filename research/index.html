Data-as-Collateral Barter Tokenization

Core academic paper. Proposes that refugees can "farm" blockchain transaction fees by contributing behavioral data (cooperation patterns, agricultural knowledge, carbon verification) as collateral for ETH-backed barter tokens.

Gap addressed: No existing model solves KYC-free access + zero transaction costs + data sovereignty simultaneously.
Method: Mixed-methods pilot with 500 refugees in Nakivale. HEROES behavioral detection for identity, data monetization for costs, 150% overcollateralized barter tokens.
Key finding: Refugees can earn $1.50-3.50/month in data value - enough to cover blockchain fees (180x cost coverage) while building portable on-chain reputation.

DAO Governance for Refugee NGOs

Can decentralized governance work in low-connectivity refugee settlements? Tests whether cooperation scores can replace token-weighted voting, and whether cryptographic verification can replace expensive third-party audits.

5 research questions: Telegram-first governance, meritocratic voting, cryptographic certification, reputation staking, self-sustainability.
Grounded in: 8 years of Unidos field data, Ostrom commons governance principles, Mondragon cooperative model.
Timeline: 4-phase roadmap (Q1 2026 Foundation through Q3 2027 Launch).

Multi-Agent Governance (RACI-V Protocol)

Live experiment testing structured AI coordination vs unstructured "chaos" using 3 real Telegram accounts. Addresses 7 of 8 failure modes identified by Hadfield & Koh (arXiv:2509.01063) in multi-agent economic systems.

Literature anchor: Hadfield & Koh identify 8 AI governance problems theoretically - zero experiments. This is the first live test.
Method: Within-subjects design. Same 3 actors, same questions, two conditions (chaos vs RACI-V). Oracle validation rate as primary metric. All Telegram message IDs logged for independent verification.
Hypothesis: RACI-V produces higher answer quality (d=0.42 in simulation) and lower variance than chaos, even with identical underlying LLMs.
Members Only Live Experiment Target: ACM EC / AAAI / ICAIF

HEROES Behavioral Detection

5-stage fraud prevention framework using 13 behavioral indicators (cooperation patterns, not identity documents). Combined with ZK proofs, enables KYC-free identity that protects refugees from surveillance while proving trustworthiness.

Why behavioral, not biometric: Video/iris verification risks government surveillance of displaced populations. Behavioral data (do they help others? do they follow through?) measures trust without exposing identity.
32 CPI signals across 10 dimensions, longitudinally tracked. Early modeling: $500/month sustainability threshold per community.
Literature gap: Existing systems (Proof of Humanity, Gitcoin Passport) use input-based credentials, not behavior-based trust.

Reputation Staking Model

Award-winning NGOs stake their on-chain reputation to vet newcomer projects across Africa. Unidos' 6 awards (UNHCR, Ockenden, Lush, UNHCR Innovation Fund) become verifiable trust signals that transfer to vetted organizations.

Mechanism: Awards = external jury validation = on-chain reputation points. Unidos stakes reputation when vouching for new NGOs. If vetted NGO fails, Unidos' score decreases (graduated slashing, 5-100%).
Scale target: 100+ refugee-led organizations across Africa by 2030, each vetting the next generation.
Innovation: Social capital becomes programmable. Trust compounds like soil health - annually, measurably.
Members Only Complete Target: Stanford Social Innovation Review

Refugee Data Marketplace

Survey-as-a-service model where refugees are paid directly for data contributions ($5/response) rather than intermediaries ($10/response via consultants). Targets NGO research budgets: UNHCR $50M+/year, IRC $15M/year in research spending.

Revenue model: 80% grants + 15% Euler yield + 5% data fees = $21/month per participant (above $10/month sustainability target).
Data types: Agricultural knowledge (soil, yield, techniques), carbon sequestration verification, cooperation behavioral patterns, market price data.
Privacy: ZK-verified data commons - buyers access aggregate insights, never individual identities.

How the Research Connects

Each paper addresses a different layer of the same problem: can the middle class finance itself without intermediaries?

Core Paper

Data-as-Collateral is the central thesis. Every other paper provides a building block that makes the model work.

The academic paper needs each layer to function. Without behavioral identity, there is no KYC-free access. Without governance, there is no accountability. Without reputation, there is no trust bridge to new communities.

Governance Layer - RACI-V Protocol

Provides the coordination mechanism. Hadfield & Koh (2025) identify 8 ways multi-agent systems fail. RACI-V addresses 7 of them with structured roles (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed, Verified). Live experiment running on Telegram.

Identity Layer - HEROES Detection

Provides KYC-free behavioral identity. 13 cooperation indicators measured over time replace document-based verification. ZK proofs protect privacy while proving trustworthiness to data buyers and governance systems.

Trust Layer - Reputation Staking

Provides the bridge between established NGOs and newcomers. Unidos' 6 international awards become programmable trust that can be staked, transferred, and verified on-chain. Graduated slashing prevents rubber-stamping.

Implementation Layer - DAO Governance

Provides the 4-phase roadmap from traditional NGO to decentralized governance. Tested against Ostrom's 8 principles for governing the commons and Mondragon's 70-year cooperative track record.

Market Layer - Data Marketplace

Provides the revenue engine. Refugees contribute data, earn income, fund the system. $50M+ in annual NGO research spending is the addressable market. Community retains sovereignty over its own data.

Academic Context and Key References

This research sits at the intersection of three fields: refugee financial inclusion, decentralized governance, and AI coordination. Below are the primary sources that ground each research track.

  • Hadfield & Koh (2025) - "An Economy of AI Agents" (arXiv:2509.01063). Taxonomy of 8 failure modes in multi-agent economic systems. Theoretical only - our RACI-V experiment provides the first live test.
  • Betts et al. (2022) - Systematic exclusion of refugees from financial services. Survey of 5,000 refugees: 94% want bank accounts, 18% succeed. Regulatory barriers, not discrimination.
  • Pentland et al. (2023) - "New Deal on Data." Users valued data privacy at $5-20/month in Kenya pilot. Foundation for our data-as-collateral pricing model.
  • Lanier & Weyl (2018) - Data dignity movement. Collective bargaining yields $20-50/user/year vs individual negotiation. Informs our community-level data marketplace design.
  • Ruddick et al. (2015) - Community Inclusion Currencies in Kenya: 15% trade increase, 22% food security improvement over 4 years with 10,000 users. Paper-based limitation we solve with blockchain.
  • Ostrom (1990) - 8 design principles for governing commons. Our DAO governance model maps directly to each principle.
  • Mondragon Cooperative (1956-present) - 70+ years of cooperative governance with 80,000+ worker-owners. Our benchmark for long-term viability.
